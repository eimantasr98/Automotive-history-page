Aixam

Aixam is a French car manufacturer that was founded in 1983. The company is best known for producing small, microcars that are popular in Europe.
Aixam's history can be traced back to the early 1980s, when the French government introduced a new set of regulations governing the production of small cars. These regulations were designed to encourage the development of environmentally friendly vehicles, and Aixam saw an opportunity to create a line of microcars that would meet these requirements.
In 1983, the first Aixam model was released: the Aixam 400. This car was powered by a small, two-cylinder engine and had a top speed of just 45 kilometers per hour. Despite its slow speed, the Aixam 400 was a popular choice for city dwellers who needed a compact, affordable car for short trips.
Over the next few years, Aixam continued to develop and improve its microcar line. In 1986, the company introduced the Aixam 500, which featured a more powerful engine and a higher top speed of 65 kilometers per hour. This model was followed by the Aixam Crossline in 1991, which was the first Aixam car to feature four-wheel drive.
In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Aixam expanded its product line to include electric cars. The first electric Aixam model was the E-City, which was released in 1998. This car had a range of up to 60 kilometers on a single charge and was popular with environmentally conscious consumers.
Today, Aixam continues to produce a wide range of microcars and electric vehicles. The company's cars are known for their compact size, affordability, and low emissions, making them a popular choice for urban residents in Europe. Despite its humble beginnings, Aixam has become a major player in the European car market and continues to innovate and grow.
