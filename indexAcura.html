Automotive History

Acura

Acura is a luxury vehicle brand that was introduced by the Japanese automaker Honda in 1986. The brand was created to provide a more upscale option for Honda's customers, and it quickly became known for its high-quality, reliable vehicles.
The first Acura model to be introduced was the Acura Legend, a luxury sedan that was unveiled in 1986. This model was followed by the Acura Integra, a compact sports car that was first introduced in 1987. Both of these models were well-received and helped to establish Acura as a reputable luxury vehicle brand.
In the 1990s, Acura continued to expand its lineup with the introduction of several new models. These included the Acura NSX, a high-performance sports car, and the Acura RL, a luxury sedan. The NSX was known for its advanced technology and engineering, while the RL was praised for its refined interior and smooth ride.
In the 2000s, Acura introduced several new models, including the Acura MDX, a popular luxury SUV, and the Acura TSX, a compact luxury sedan. These models helped to further solidify Acura's reputation as a leading luxury vehicle brand.
Today, Acura continues to produce a wide range of luxury vehicles, including sedans, SUVs, and sports cars. The brand is known for its focus on advanced technology and engineering, and its commitment to providing high-quality vehicles to its customers.
Overall, the history of Acura is one of growth and success. From its humble beginnings as a luxury vehicle brand within Honda, Acura has become a respected and well-known name in the automotive industry.
